      Name

      ڼ (Arabic Letter Noon With Ring, U+06BC) is an extended Arabic letter used to represent a distinct sound not found in classical Arabic. Visually, it is derived from the standard Arabic letter Noon (ن) but features a small circular diacritic, or "ring," positioned above it, replacing the single dot of the regular Noon. This character primarily represents a retroflex nasal consonant sound and is crucial for the accurate orthography of several languages that utilize the Arabic script, such as Sindhi, Saraiki, and Pashto. Its inclusion in Unicode highlights the adaptability of the Arabic script to accommodate the unique phonological inventories of various non-Arabic languages.
